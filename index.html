Resistance Spot Welding Simulation of Aluminum 6061-T6 sheets in presence of Mild Steel sheets

2021

shiraz University, Shiraz, Fars Province, Iran

assined for bachelor final project by Dr. Rozar anjabin

  • RSW is widely used for joining aluminum sheets in automotive and aerospace industries, but defects like expulsion and weld nugget cracking can occur due to aluminum's high conductivity. Welding parameter optimization is required to improve weld quality. ABAQUS software can simulate the effect of mild steel sheets on the welding process and optimize parameters by varying welding current and time. This study can minimize defects and increase joint strength, improving the quality of resistance spot welding of T6-6061 aluminum sheets.

Microstructure, corrosion and tribological performance of multilayered AA5083 fabricated by modified friction stir deposition – additive manufacturing

2023

Amirkabir University of Technology (Tehran Polytechnique), Tehran, Tehran Province, Iran

Submitted in 2023

  • Abstract: Modified Friction Stir Deposition – Additive Manufacturing (MFSD-AM) as an alternate method for Friction Stir Deposition (FSD) was utilized to produce aluminum alloy Additive Manufacture Products (AMPs). The study designates the effect of MFSD-AM process parameters on macrostructure, microstructure evolution, hardness, wear behavior, and corrosion resistance of AMPs in order to attempt further enhancement of tribological and corrosion properties. Macrostructure and microstructure evolution observations of the AMPs were performed using optical microscopy. Wear characteristics of AMPs were inspected using a pin on disc wear tester at the room temperature. Microstructural observations of worn out surfaces were carried out using Scanning Electron Microscopy (SEM). Hardness of AMPs were measured by a vickers hardness tester. Corrosion resistance was investigated using Potentiodynamic polarization test. substantial grain refinement around # to #% was achieved inside the Stir Zone (SZ). AMPs showed 9 to 15% growth in hardness compared to wrought material. AMPs of specimens number 5 and 6 showed a preferable wear resistance compared to as received consumables. Predominant wear mechanism of the as received consumable was identified as severe adhesive wear, which changed to abrasive and adhecive wear in AMPs. Increase in corrosion resistance compared to as received consumable was observed to a slight extent for AMPs. AMPs did not display a passivation range due to fragmentation and dissolution of Intermetallic Compounds (IMCs).
  • Key words: Metal additive manufacturing; Friction stir deposition; Aluminum matrix composite; Wear behavior; Corrosion resistance.
